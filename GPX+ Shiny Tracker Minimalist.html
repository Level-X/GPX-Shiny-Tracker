Information on the Minimalist Edition
  • This version contains mostly one Pokédex entry per evolution line on Global PokédeX Plus with the exception of:
    • Pokémon that require DNA Splicers. This is due to the rarity of the item, it's probably easier to re-hunt a Pokémon than using the DNA Splicer method.
    • Alternate Arceus forms, due to the method of obtaining the species.
    • Sawsbuck can change its forms, but Deerling can't. If you want to obtain all Pokédex entries you'd need all Deerling anyway, so all Sawsbuck forms are included.
    • Whether Toxtricity evolves into [Amped] or [Low Key] form depends on its Nature, so you'll still need one of the forms after you've obtained Toxtricity [Gigantamax].
  • To mark a Pokémon as "owned" you can use the JavaScript feature by left-clicking on its icon.
  • Hover over icons to see their Pokédex number, species name, current form, typing, and Pokédex requirements.
    • Under the "Pokédex requirements" section, the ⚠️ icon represents that the species is missable.
  • National Pokédex order takes priority in the sorting of entries, including Novelty Pokémon.
  • Pokémon that haven't been released use placeholder data, which will be updated in the future.
  • Pokémon that have multiple forms such as Rotom use its base icon, while something like Thundurus will use its Therian Form icon.
    • If a Pokémon has a Novelty form that can be reversed, such as Ditto [Book], Wurmple [Party Hat], Rotom [Dex], Vanillite [Snowless], Greninja [Ash], Vivillon [Love Pattern] or Nihilego [Parasite], its official form will be used over its Novelty counterpart.
    • If a Pokémon has a Novelty form that can't be reversed, such as: Cherrim [Frost], Cherrim [Damp], Cherrim [Heat], Mismagius [Master of Illusions], Vanillishake, and Melonait, both its official form and its Novelty counterpart are used.

Stuck and need some help? Check out the Getting Started page!
